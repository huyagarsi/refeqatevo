Who Inherited Chicks Member Laura Lynchs Huge Estate After The Musicians Sudden Death?

August 2024 · 6 minute read

Highlights

  • Laura Lynch, a founding member of The Chicks, tragically passed away in a car accident at the age of 65.
  • While her net worth is unknown, it is likely that Lynch left her money to her husband, Mac Tull, and their daughter, Asia.
  • Lynch's will and estate were likely straightforward, as she only had one child and had prepared for her death.

The Dixie Chicks (now just called The Chicks) made their mark in the world of country music long ago, and they have continued to succeed ever since. The Chicks are as famous for their political views as they are for songs like "Landslide." Like Dolly Parton, the Chicks members speak their minds about what matters to them. They don't seem to mind if people disagree with them, which is admirable.

At the end of December 2023, Laura Lynch, one of the founding members of the Chicks, tragically died. Since she was part of the popular band for a little while, fans are curious about who received the money that she left behind.

The Chicks Founding Member Laura Lynch's Family Likely Inherited Her Estate

While there has been no official confirmation, Laura Lynch likely left her money to her husband, Mac Tull, and their daughter, Asia. Since Lynch's cousin Mick Lynch is the one who shared the sad news of her passing with media outlets, it's possible that they were close and she left him some money as well.

Laura Lynch's net worth is unknown. But since she was part of the Dixie Chicks when they first began making music, it seems likely that she made some good money. The Chicks are some of the wealthiest country musicians, and according to Fox Business, their net worth is $150 million.

If someone prepares for their death and sets up a will and trust, then it's easier for their relatives to deal with the question of who inherits their estate afterward. According to Willfill.co, sometimes things don't work out quite that easily. For example, when Anna Nicole Smith passed away, Daniel, her child, inherited her estate. But her daughter, Dannielynn, wasn't included in the will since Smith didn't mention what would happen if she had more kids. However, in the case of Laura Lynch, she had one child. It seems likely that her will and estate were straightforward.

Forbes also mentioned what happened to James Gandolfini's estate. His wife only received 20% of the actor's estate, and 55% of the estate was used for taxes. While there is no way of knowing how much of her estate Lynch left to her husband, if she left a larger portion than Gandolfini did, then this situation would have been avoided.

According to Myplainview.com, Lynch decided to leave The Chicks (then known as The Dixie Chucks) in 1995. At that time, she wanted to focus on being a mom to Asia, and they began living in Mineral Wells. The publication reported that in 1997, Laura Lynch married Mac Tull. They used to be friends and they reconnected thanks to the fact that they were both at an open house. When interviewed by Myplainview.com, Lynch explained that while the current members of the Chicks were critical of former president George Bush, she didn't agree with them.

It seems like Laura Lynch embraced being outside the spotlight once she decided that she didn't want to be in the band anymore. She seems to have lived a nice, quiet with her family.

What Was The Cause Of The Chicks Founding Member Laura Lynch's Death?

According to The Hollywood Reporter, Laura Lynch died in a car accident when she was 65 years old. A truck drove right into her car when she was driving on a highway close to El Paso, Texas. While the driver of the second car was hurt and went to the hospital, she died right away, which is incredibly tragic. The publication found out the details from a report from the Texas Department of Safety.

According to The New York Post, @mavmoto182 shared a TikTok video and said that Lynch's car was on fire as a result of the accident. According to this video, someone who was driving nearby cut her seatbelt off and took her out of the car. Mick Lynch, Laura Lynch's cousin, shared that she was on her way to Dell City, Texas.

A few days later, the New York Post reported that the truck driver could be charged for Lynch's death. At the time of this writing, there is an investigation, but no updates.

According to Us Weekly, Emily Strayer, Martie Maguire, and Robin Lynn Macy shared their sadness over Lynch's death in an Instagram post on December 23rd, 2023.

Jerry Clower Full Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Career Beginnings

Howard Gerald Jerry Clower, born in Liberty, Mississippi, embarked on a journey that would see him rise from humble beginnings to become a beloved figure in American comedy.

His early life, marked by service as a Navy Radioman Third Class during World War II, laid a strong foundation for his character and work ethic.

His educational pursuits at Mississippi State University, where he studied agriculture and played college football, further enriched his understanding of rural Southern life—a theme that would become central to his comedy.

A Seed Salesman Turned Comedy Star

After college, his career path took a turn from agriculture to entertainment, starting in the unlikely role of a fertilizer salesman.

His knack for storytelling, initially a tool to enhance sales, eventually caught the attention of Edwin Big Ed Wilkes and Bud Andrews.

This pivotal moment led to Clower making recordings of his stories, culminating in The Coon Hunt, which earned a platinum record for sales exceeding $1 million.

The Rise to Fame

His authentic and relatable storytelling resonated with audiences far and wide, leading to a contract with MCA Records.

His debut album, Jerry Clower from Yazoo City, Mississippi Talkin’, achieved remarkable success, staying in the top 20 on the country charts for 30 weeks and selling over a million dollars worth of records in just 10 months.

This success marked the beginning of Clower’s rise to fame, establishing him as a household name in comedy.

Contribution to Comedy and Culture

Throughout his 27-year career, Clower made significant contributions to comedy and Southern culture.

He produced 27 full-length recordings, sharing tales of the Ledbetters and other quintessential Southern characters.

His work was not just about making people laugh; it was a celebration of Southern life, traditions, and values.

Clower’s involvement with the Grand Ole Opry, his co-hosting of the Country Crossroads radio show, and appearances on various television programs further cemented his legacy as a pioneer of Southern comedy.

Faith, Family, and Legacy

A devout Christian, Clower often intertwined his faith with his humor, offering a unique blend of comedy that was both entertaining and inspirational.

FAQs about Jerry Clower

What is Jerry Clower famous for?

He is famous for his storytelling comedy, particularly his tales about the fictional Ledbetter family. His humor, steeped in Southern culture, resonated with a wide audience, making him a beloved figure in both comedy and country music circles.

When was he born?

He was born on September 28, 1926.

Where was Jerry Clower from?

He was born in Liberty, Mississippi, USA, and his comedy often reflected the lifestyle and culture of the rural South.

What was his most popular story or joke?

One of his most famous stories is The Coon Hunt, which was instrumental in launching his career. This story, like many of his others, is a humorous account of a misadventure with friends in the countryside.

Did he have any other professions besides comedy?

Yes, before becoming a comedian, Clower was a salesman for the Mississippi Chemical Corporation. His natural storytelling ability was discovered through the sales presentations and speeches he gave in this role.

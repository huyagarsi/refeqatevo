Danny Masterson was recently handed a 30-year jail sentence for two cases of sexual assault. But even though he’s likely going to be in jail for decades over violent crimes, his wife Bijou Phillips reportedly has no plans to leave him.

“She has had a very difficult time since the conviction,” a source divulged to PEOPLE. “She loves Danny and has no plans to file for divorce. She will be by his side for it all.”

Bijou Plans to Stay With Danny As He Appeals His Conviction

The insider added that Bijou, an actress known for role on the television program Raising Hope, among many roles, has been relying on her family and friends since Dany’s legal troubles began. “She is in touch with his lawyer,” they continued. “They are planning appeals. She tries to stay hopeful.”

Bijou, along with Masterson's family members, was present in the courtroom during Thursday's sentencing. In a courtroom sketch, the actor was observed blowing a kiss to his wife just before being escorted to prison.

While Bijou may be working with her husband and his legal team to explore their options, an earlier report said the actress was shocked when he was given a three-decade sentence.

“She wasn’t prepared for the verdict,” a source said in June, after Danny was found guilty of two counts of rape. “She never expected him to be found guilty. She couldn’t believe that he was taken into custody right away and remanded.” At the time, the source said Danny’s wife was “terrified about the sentencing.”

However, the source echoed the recent report’s sentiment that Bijou won’t be leaving her husband, despite the conviction. “No matter what happens though, Bijou supports him,” they went on. “She has no plans to leave him. Bijou, 43, and Danny, 47, have been married since 2011.

In 2020, Masterson faced charges of forcibly raping three women in separate incidents between 2001 and 2003. Following his arrest, he remained free on a $3.3 million bail. Then, in January 2021, he entered a not guilty plea to all three charges.

Last June, he was found guilty on two of the counts, while the third count led to a hung jury, with eight out of the jurors voting in favor of convicting the actor.

Many Famous Faces Requested A Lenient Sentencing For Danny

Despite many famous faces writing to the court in support of Danny getting a lenient conviction, the That ‘70s Show star still received a decades-long sentence earlier this week. However, many celebrities who defended him are now facing backlash post-sentencing, including his former co-stars Mila Kunis and Ashton Kutcher.

The couple both submitted letters of support for Danny to the court while his sentencing was being considered, in the hope of achieving a more lenient outcome.

“As a friend, Danny has been nothing but a positive influence on me,” Ashton, 45, wrote in his note. “Over [a] 25-year relationship I don’t ever recall him lying to me,” Ashton went on. “He’s taught me about being direct and confronting issues in life and relationships head-on, resolving them, and moving forward.”

Ashton also referenced how Danny is a “hands-on” dad to the 9-year old daughter he shares with Bijou Phillips, writing, “I do not believe he is an ongoing harm to society and having his daughter raised without a present father would [be] a tertiary injustice in and of itself.”

Career

Halsey used to post covers and songs on various social media. She became famous for a parody of Taylor Swift’s song “I Knew You Were Trouble.” She chose Halsey as her stage name as it is an anagram of her first name, and also refers to the Halsey Street station of the New York City Subway in Brooklyn, where she has spent considerable time as a teenager.

After her song “Ghost” that she had released on SoundCloud did well in the alternative charts just the day after release, many companies contacted her. She ended up signing with Astralwerks as she thought they gave her the most creative freedom. She released her debut extended play, Room 93, on October 27, 2014.

Halsey debuted with her first album “Badlands” on August 28, 2015, with four singles “Ghost,” “New Americana,” “Colors,” and “Castle.” “Gasoline” became one of the most famous songs despite not being single. Castle was rerecorded to promote the movie “The Huntsman: Winter’s War.” On July 29, 2016, Halsey’s collaboration with The Chainsmokers’  “Closer” released and became a huge success.

Halsey released her second studio album, “Hopeless Fountain Kingdom,” on June 2 with the single “Now or Never” and promotional singles “Eyes Closed” and “Strangers.” The second single from the album was “Bad at Love.”

On October 4, Halsey released a single titled “Without Me,” her first single through Capitol Records. “Boy with Luv” by South Korean boy band BTS featuring her was released on April 12. She then proceeded to release another single “Nightmare” on May 17. Manic, her third studio album was released on January 17, 2020, with singles “Graveyard,” “Clementine,” “Finally // Beautiful Stranger,” “Suga’s Interlude,” and “You Should Be Sad.”

Awards & Achievements

Halsey has been awarded quite a few awards from iHeartRadio Music Awards, Billboard Music Awards, Teen Choice Awards, American Music Awards, Global Awards, and much more. Some notable awards are Billboard Women in Music: Rising Star (2016), GLAAD Media Awards: Outstanding Music Artist (2018), and Songwriters Hall of Fame: Hal David Starlight Award (2019).

She also entered the Guinness World Records with BTS in 2019 for Most viewed YouTube music video in 24 hours by a K-pop group, Most viewed YouTube music video in 24 hours and Most viewed YouTube video in 24 hours.

Who is Carlos Oher, the brother of Michael Oher?

June 2024 · 4 minute read

Carlos Oher is the sibling of Michael Oher, a former American football offensive tackle with an eight-season tenure in the National Football League (NFL).

The remarkable life journey of Michael Oher served as the source of inspiration for the book and subsequent film adaptation, “The Blind Side.” This narrative depicted Michael’s triumph over homelessness and destitution, thanks to the assistance of a prosperous white family, the Tuohys, who took him under their wing.

Nonetheless, a recent revelation by Carlos Oher has shed light on a different perspective.

Carlos claims that the Tuohys never formally adopted him or his brother, alleging that they leveraged their story for financial gain.

In the following article, we will delve into Carlos Oher’s background and present circumstances, as well as provide insights into his renowned sibling, Michael Oher.

Is Michael Oher A Millionaire?

Michael Oher achieved remarkable success in the NFL, amassing substantial wealth through lucrative contracts and endorsements.

As of 2023, Celebrity Net Worth estimates his net worth to be approximately $20 million.

Oher’s NFL journey included stints with three teams: the Baltimore Ravens, the Tennessee Titans, and the Carolina Panthers.

His crowning achievement came in 2013 when he earned a Super Bowl ring as a member of the Ravens, and he further showcased his skills by earning a Pro Bowl nomination in 2015.

Regrettably, Oher decided to retire from professional football in 2017 due to the multiple concussions he had endured during his career.

Is Michael Oher Still Involved With The Tuohys?

Michael Oher’s relationship with the Tuohys, who provided him with support during his teenage years and helped him pursue both education and football, has been quite complex.

He has conveyed feelings of gratitude and affection towards them, alongside sentiments of resentment and frustration stemming from their portrayal of him in “The Blind Side.”

Oher has voiced his belief that the movie exaggerated his limited football knowledge and academic skills, painting him as a charity case.

Furthermore, Oher has accused the Tuohys of exploiting him financially by initiating a conservatorship, which granted them control over his assets and income.

To address this matter, he has taken legal action by filing a petition to terminate the conservatorship and reclaim his finances from them.

The Tuohys, in response, have refuted Oher’s allegations.

They assert that their intentions were solely geared toward safeguarding him from potential exploitation by others.

Despite the disputes, they have maintained that they still regard him as a beloved member of their family, expressing a hope for reconciliation in the future.

When Did Michael Oher Retire?

In 2017, Michael Oher concluded his NFL career after an impressive eight-season run.

His retirement was made public via an Instagram post, in which he expressed gratitude for his career and his desire to prioritize his well-being and happiness.

Oher’s final season with the Panthers had been marred by the impact of multiple concussions, which significantly hampered both his on-field performance and mental state.

This unfortunate situation led to his placement on injured reserve in November 2016, and subsequently, he was released from the Panthers’ roster in July 2017.

Where Did Michael Oher End Up?

Following his retirement from football, Michael Oher has deliberately maintained a discreet presence, steering clear of the media spotlight.

Information regarding his current location and endeavors remains elusive.

Nonetheless, he sporadically shares insights into his post-football life through social media.

These glimpses include photographs of his travels to various destinations like Hawaii, Mexico, and Jamaica. Moreover, he has taken to posting motivational messages and inspirational quotes, reflecting on his personal journey and offering encouragement to those facing their own challenges.

Carlos Oher, sibling to former NFL luminary Michael Oher, whose compelling life saga served as the inspiration for “The Blind Side,” asserts that he and his brother were never formally adopted by the Tuohys, the individuals portrayed as their benefactors in the film.

Carlos further contends that the Tuohys allegedly took advantage of them by initiating a conservatorship, potentially motivated by financial interests.

Michael Oher’s relationship with the Tuohys has been complex, oscillating between expressions of affection and resentment.

In 2017, he concluded his football career due to the toll of concussions, thereafter opting for a more secluded existence with sporadic glimpses into his life shared on social media.

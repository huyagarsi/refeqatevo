What Happened To Lucy Gray In Hunger Games: Find Out More!

May 2024 · 4 minute read

One of the most important mysteries from both the book and the movie The Hunger Games: The Ballad of Songbirds & Snakes is what will happen to Lucy Gray Baird. In the film version of Suzanne Collins’ prequel novel, Rachel Zegler’s portrayal of District 12’s female tribute is crucial to the growth of a young Coriolanus Snow (Tom Blyth).

After making it through the games in the Capitol, the gifted vocalist and technical victor of the 10th Hunger Games is free to live her life with the Coveys once more. But the last scenes of Snow cast doubt on the apparently contented and complete life she ought to be able to lead.

Hunger Games Sequel 

The prequel narrative narrates the tale of the girl, who was not given a name at the time, who was mentioned as District 12’s first victor in the original Hunger Games trilogy. This was brought about by the erasure of Lucy Gray and the 10th Hunger Games from history upon their completion.

By the time The Hunger Games: The Ballad of Songbirds & Snakes ends, both the world’s characters and the audience are left wondering what happened to Lucy Gray Baird. In this sense, the film stays fairly true to the original work, although it does not make the solution any clearer.

What Happened To Lucy GrayIn Hunger Games 

In The Ballad of Songbirds & Snakes, Lucy Gray’s destiny is purposefully left open-ended, allowing viewers to extrapolate their own conclusions. This occurs because Lucy tries to escape Snow in a downpour after they decide to escape the Districts, but she finds out that he killed Sejanus Plinth while they were traveling.

After discovering the guns that could prove he was guilty of his past misdeeds, Snow decides he can destroy them and go back to the glamorous life in the Capitol without having to deal with these unresolved issues, only for Lucy to imply that she might be one. This results in a confrontation in the forest where Coriolanus Snow, having been bitten by a snake, shoots at Lucy Gray.

The Ballad of Songbirds & Snakes depicts Lucy explicitly as yelling and falling after being shot at by Snow, but when Snow reaches her suspected location, her body is nowhere to be seen. Eventually, even her footprints in the mud vanish, leaving Snow as perplexed as to what had happened to her. The song “The Hanging Tree” is then heard by Snow, though it is purposefully unclear if she was actively singing it at first or if the noise was caused by Jabberjays nearby.

The Ballad of Songbirds & Snakes leaves open the subject of whether Lucy Gray passes away. During their last meeting, Casca Highbottom does inform Snow that he has information from sources indicating that Lucy has “disappeared,” which sparks rumors that the mayor of District 12 murdered her following the death of his daughter.

The way Lucy Gray responds to Snow’s inquiries regarding whether or not she survives and the lyrics of her song about herself point to an unclear conclusion. Although Lucy calls herself a “survivor,” she also implies that “it’s a mystery” as to whether or not she actually lives in the song. The precursor to The Hunger Games does likewise.
